# Privacy Policy

**Effective date:** 2 July 2026
**Last updated:** 2 July 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Robert Aneszko, a sole trader based in
Poland ("we", "us", "our"), processes personal data in connection with the
**Word Popping** mobile game ("the App").

We are the **data controller** for the purposes of the EU General Data
Protection Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2016/679, "GDPR"). You can reach
us at **robert.aneszko@gmail.com** for any privacy-related question.

---

## 1. Summary

- We do not require you to create an account and never ask for your name,
  email, or any other direct identifier.
- Your game progress is stored locally on your device. It is never uploaded
  to our servers — we operate no servers and use no analytics SDK.
- The App shows ads. **All ads are non-personalized (contextual).** We
  instruct our advertising partners to serve ads without behavioral
  profiling for every user, in every country, and we never request access
  to Apple's advertising identifier (IDFA). The App will never show you
  Apple's App Tracking Transparency prompt, because it does not track you.
- The App contains no in-app purchases.

---

## 2. Data stored locally on your device only

The following data stays on your device and is not transmitted to us:

- Your game progress (current level, booster inventory, whether tutorials
  have been shown)
- Your settings

This data is stored using Apple's standard on-device storage and is removed
when you delete the App.

## 3. Data processed by our advertising partners

Ads in the App are delivered by **Unity LevelPlay** (the ironSource
mediation platform, operated by Unity Technologies), serving ads from the
ironSource and Unity Ads networks.

We configure these partners to serve **non-personalized ads only**: we
signal "no consent to personalization" and "do not sell or share" for every
user by default, and because we never request App Tracking Transparency
permission, the advertising identifier (IDFA) is not available to them on
your device.

To deliver contextual ads, cap how often you see the same ad, prevent
fraud, and measure that an ad was shown, these partners may process:

- Your IP address (also used to derive coarse, city-level location)
- Device information (model, operating system version, language, app
  version)
- Device-generated identifiers scoped to this app's vendor (IDFV)
- Ad interaction events (impressions, clicks, video completion)
- Technical diagnostics related to ad delivery

Unity's practices are described in the
[Unity Player and App User Privacy Policy](https://unity.com/legal/game-player-and-app-user-privacy-policy).

## 4. Install attribution (Apple SKAdNetwork)

To know which ad campaigns lead to installs, the App participates in
Apple's **SKAdNetwork** framework. SKAdNetwork is designed by Apple to be
privacy-preserving: attribution is aggregated and delayed by the operating
system and does not identify you or your device to us or to ad networks.

## 5. Data processed by Apple

- **App Store analytics.** If you have opted in to sharing analytics with
  developers in iOS Settings, Apple provides us with aggregated usage
  statistics (such as install counts and retention). These are anonymous
  aggregates; we cannot identify you from them.
- **Crash diagnostics.** Apple may share crash reports with us under the
  same opt-in. They do not identify you personally.

---

## 6. Legal basis for processing

| Processing activity | Legal basis (GDPR Art. 6) |
|---|---|
| Serving non-personalized, contextual ads to fund a free app | Legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f)) |
| Fraud prevention and ad measurement by our ad partners | Legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f)) |
| Storing your progress and settings on your device | Performance of the service you request (Art. 6(1)(b)) |

We do not process personal data for behavioral advertising, profiling, or
cross-app tracking, so no consent-based processing takes place.

## 7. "Do Not Sell or Share" (US state privacy laws)

We treat **every** user as having opted out of the sale or sharing of
personal information: the "do not sell or share" signal is enabled for all
users by default. You do not need to take any action.

## 8. How long we keep data

We store no personal data on our servers. Data on your device is kept until
you delete the App. Our advertising partners retain the data described in
section 3 according to their own retention schedules, described in their
privacy notices.

## 9. Your rights under GDPR

You have the right to **access**, **rectify**, and **erase** your personal
data, to **restrict** or **object to** processing, and to **lodge a
complaint** with a supervisory authority.

Because we hold no personal data about you on any server, the practical way
to exercise erasure is to delete the App — this permanently removes all
locally stored data. For anything else, contact
**robert.aneszko@gmail.com**, and we will assist, including forwarding
requests to our advertising partners where applicable.

You can lodge a complaint with the Polish data protection authority:

> Urząd Ochrony Danych Osobowych (UODO)
> ul. Stawki 2, 00-193 Warszawa, Poland
> <https://uodo.gov.pl>

## 10. International transfers

Our advertising partner (Unity Technologies, including its ironSource
platform) and Apple operate globally, including in the United States.
Where personal data is transferred outside the EEA, these transfers rely
on the EU-US Data Privacy Framework and/or Standard Contractual Clauses,
as required by GDPR Articles 45–46.

## 11. Children

The App is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly
collect personal data from children. Because all ads are non-personalized
for every user, no user — of any age — receives behaviorally targeted
advertising. If you believe a child has provided personal data through the
App, contact us and we will assist.

## 12. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the
top reflects the most recent revision. Material changes will be
communicated through the App where reasonably possible.
